Interesting Facts about Camels

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.
  3. Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

Interesting Facts about Chameleons

  1. Chameleons are a very unique branch of the lizard group of reptiles.
  2. There are around 160 species of chameleon.
  3. Almost half of the world's chameleon species are native to Madagascar.

Interesting Facts about Pigeons

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.They can recognise each letter of the human alphabet, differentiate between photographs, and even distinguish different humans within a photograph.
  2. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
  3. Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph.

Interesting Facts about Bears

  1. There are around 180,000 to 200,000 brown bears worldwide. Most of them live in Alaska, Canada and Russia where the brown bear can still roam vast areas almost unpopulated by human beings and covered in forests.
  2. The brown bear can reach a weight of between 150 and 370 kilogrammes depending on age, sex and season.
  3. Despite their weight, the animals can cover short distances at speeds of up to 50 km/h.

Interesting Facts about Pandas

  1. Pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  2. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  3. Prehistoric pandas lived up to 2 million years ago.

Interesting Facts about Cicadas

  1. The 13- or 17-year lifespan of periodical cicadas is one of the longest of any insect.
  2. They can buzz louder than a lawnmower.
  3. Their wings repel water and bacteria
